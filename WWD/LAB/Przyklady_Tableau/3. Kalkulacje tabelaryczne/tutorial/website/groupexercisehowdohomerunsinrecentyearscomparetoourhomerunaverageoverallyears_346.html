Group Exercise: How do Home Runs in recent years compare to our Home Run Average over all years?
Let's see how Home Runs in recent years compare to previous years, by comparing the value of each year to the average across all years. Create a view that shows all our years on the x-axis, and the number of Home Runs per year on the y-axis. Let's highlight years that are above the average.

1 Navigate to the Batting Data, and drag out SUM(HR) to the Rows Shelf.

2 Drag the Year field from the Dimensions Pane to the Columns Shelf.

3 Change the view from a line chart to a bar chart in the Marks Card.

4 Fit the graph to the entire view by selecting "Entire View" in the Fit Menu.

5 Lets check the average.
Click over to the analytics pane and drag out an average line.

6 How do we reference this Average in a Calculation?
We have to create it with a Window Function!

7 Write the following equation: WINDOW_AVG(SUM([HR])), and save it as "Window Average HR".
This will take the SUM(HR) from each year, and take the average of all the values across the Window.

WINDOW_AVG(SUM([HR]))

8 Now we need a calculation to call out the years above the average.
Enter the following equation: SUM([HR])>[Window Average HR], and name it "Above Average HR?"

SUM([HR])>[Window Average HR]

9 Drop the new "Above Average HR?" field onto Color.
