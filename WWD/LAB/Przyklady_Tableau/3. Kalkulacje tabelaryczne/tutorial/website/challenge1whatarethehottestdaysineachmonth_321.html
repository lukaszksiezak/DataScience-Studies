Challenge 1: What are the hottest days in each month?
What are the hottest days in each month? In order to create this view, we will need to use the Rank function, as Sorting will not give us the correct view.
1 Navigate to the "Austin Weather" Dataset, and drag Max Temperature F to the Columns Shelf.

2 Change the aggregation from a Sum to an Average.

3 Drag the Date field to the Rows Shelf, and change the Date Part to Discrete Month.

4 Drag a new Date field from the Dimensions Shelf to the Rows Shelf, behind the MONTH(Date) pill. Change the Date Part to Discrete Day.

5 Change the view to Bars from Automatic, on the Marks Card.

6 When we press "Sort", notice that the sorting is all wrong! Drag a new "Max Temperature F" to the Detail Pane, and change the aggregation to an Average.
This failed sorting occurs because Tableau is sorting the Day of each Month at an aggregate level, before breaking down each Month's temperature difference. Notice the order of each Day of Date is the same for each Month (e.g. 19, 25, 26... for every month).

7 Change the AVG(Max Temperature F) pill on the Detail Pane to a Rank Table Calculation.

8 Right-click the new Rank Pill you've created, and change it to a Discrete Pill.

9 This allows you to drag the (now Discrete) Rank pill between the MONTH(Date) and DAY(Date) pills.

10 That's the kind of Sort we're looking for! Lastly, we need to change the Rank to Compute Pane (down).
